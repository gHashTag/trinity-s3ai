### Implications for Trinity

Trinity predicts a **normal mass hierarchy** and an effective beta-decay mass of 0.103 eV. For a normal hierarchy:
- Minimum Σm_ν ≈ 0.057 eV (from oscillations)
- DESI DR2 bound Σm_ν < 0.064 eV (95% CL) is **extremely close** to the NH minimum.
- If Σm_ν is near the lower bound (~0.06 eV), the individual masses would be roughly m₁ ≈ 0, m₂ ≈ 0.009 eV, m₃ ≈ 0.049 eV. This is **consistent** with m_β ≈ 0.05–0.06 eV, which would place it at the edge of KATRIN's final sensitivity.

**Status:** No falsification. DESI's preference for normal hierarchy is consistent with Trinity. However, if future cosmological data push Σm_ν < 0.05 eV while KATRIN measures m_β > 0.08 eV, a tension would arise.

## 12. Scientific Principle

> **A theory that cannot be falsified is not scientific.**

- **CRITICAL** (δ_CP = 65.66°) — could invalidate a major sector
