### Trinity Position on Dark Matter

Trinity-s3ai does **not** predict a standard WIMP. The framework's dark matter candidate is:

- **Spectral residue**: Zero modes or near-zero modes of the Dirac operator that do not couple to the SM gauge fields.
- **600-cell lattice defects**: Topological defects in the discrete geometry that behave as stable relics.
- **Non-thermal production**: Generated during the discrete-geometry phase transition, not freeze-out.

**This is not a standard prediction.** LZ/XENONnT measures WIMP-nucleon scattering cross-sections. If they see nothing (null result), Trinity is **not falsified** — it is consistent. If they see a standard WIMP signal, Trinity would need revision.

### Annual Modulation

Trinity **does** predict an annual modulation in the spectral residue density, tied to the Earth's motion through the 600-cell lattice orientation. However:

- Amplitude: **< 1%** (unobservably small)
- Phase: Uncertain (depends on lattice alignment)
- **This is not a robust, testable prediction.** It is a qualitative feature.

| Result | Verdict |
|--------|---------|
| Null result (σ < 10⁻⁴⁷ cm²) | ✅ Consistent (Trinity predicts no WIMP) |
| Discovery (standard WIMP) | ❌ **INCONSISTENT** (requires revision) |
| Annual modulation at >3σ | ⚠️ Requires careful analysis |

| **DUNE precision** | ±10° (2028), ±5° (2032) |

### This is the Riskiest Trinity Prediction

δ_CP = 65.66° is the **critical-risk** prediction in the Trinity registry. It is:

- **Mathematically fixed**: derived from H4 Coxeter geometry, not adjustable.
- **In strong tension** with current global fits (~177°).
- **Binary testable**: DUNE will give a clear answer.

### Falsification Criteria

| DUNE Result (2028–2032) | Verdict |
|------------------------|---------|
| 50° – 80° | ✅ **CONFIRMED** (Trinity validated) |
| 80° – 100° | ⚠️ INCONCLUSIVE (wait for precision) |
| < 30° or > 100° | ❌ **FALSIFIED** (PMNS sector invalidated) |

### Impact if Falsified

If DUNE measures δ_CP outside 30°–100°, the H₄ → PMNS mapping is incorrect. **This does not invalidate the entire framework**, but it requires:

1. Revision of the neutrino sector geometry.
2. Possible abandonment of the H₄-based PMNS construction.
3. Re-evaluation of all neutrino-related formulas (masses, mixings).

Other sectors (Higgs, quark masses, strong CP) are **unaffected**.

## 8. Scientific Principle

> **A theory that cannot be falsified is not scientific.**

Trinity makes predictions at three risk levels:
- **CRITICAL** (δ_CP = 65.66°) — could invalidate a major sector
- **HIGH** (m_νe = 0.103 eV) — would require significant revision
- **LOW** (sin²θ₁₃ = 0.0220) — minor adjustment needed

This distribution is deliberate. If all predictions were "safe," Trinity would be curve-fitting. The 5.6σ tension on δ_CP is **precisely what makes this interesting**.
