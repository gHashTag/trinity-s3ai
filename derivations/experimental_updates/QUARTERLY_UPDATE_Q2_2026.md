## 2. What Changed This Quarter?

### 2.1 KATRIN — 1000 Days Complete (April 2026)

- **Milestone:** KATRIN celebrated 1000 days of tritium β-decay data at a symposium at KIT on **17 April 2026**.
- **Published limit:** m_νe < **0.45 eV** (90% CL, *Science* 388, 180–185, April 2025) from campaigns 1–5 (259 days).
- **Outlook:** Analysis of the full 1000-day dataset is ongoing. The collaboration projects a final sensitivity of **< 0.3 eV** (90% CL).
- **Trinity relevance:** Trinity predicts m_νe = **0.103 eV**. The current upper limit is ~4× above this value. No tension yet, but the prediction is now within a factor of ~3 of the projected final bound.
- **Next phase:** TRISTAN (sterile-neutrino search) begins 2026; KATRIN++ R&D starts 2029.

### 2.2 Belle II — World Luminosity Record (March 2026)

- **Record:** SuperKEKB achieved **5.24 × 10³⁴ cm⁻²s⁻¹** on **19 March 2026** — the highest instantaneous luminosity ever recorded at a particle accelerator.
- **Dataset:** Total integrated luminosity reached **~763 fb⁻¹** by May 2026 (Run 1 + Run 2).
- **New result:** The B⁺ → K⁺νν̄ analysis (*Phys. Rev. D* 112, 092016, 2025) reports a branching fraction of (2.4 ± 0.5 ± 0.5) × 10⁻⁵, **2.7σ above the SM expectation** and 3.5σ above background-only. This is a hint of new physics in flavor-changing neutral currents, though not yet significant.
- **Trinity relevance:** Trinity does not predict quark-sector flavor observables, but confirmed BSM flavor physics would raise the bar for any beyond-SM framework.

### 2.3 JUNO — Extended Data Taking

- JUNO’s first 59.1-day result (sin²θ₁₂, Δm²₂₁) was published in **November 2025**. Since then the detector has continued stable operation.
- **Trinity cross-check:** Trinity predicts Δm²₂₁ = **7.53 × 10⁻⁵ eV²**. JUNO measures **(7.50 ± 0.12) × 10⁻⁵ eV²** — a **0.25σ match**. This is the first direct test of a Trinity oscillation parameter, and it **passes**.
- **Next:** Sub-percent precision on Δm²₂₁ and sin²θ₁₂ expected with ~1–2 years of data.

### 2.4 DUNE — Construction on Track

- Far detector cryostat installation is proceeding; FD Module 1 installation remains on schedule for **mid-2027**.
- PIP-II accelerator completion: **end of 2029**.
- Non-beam physics (atmospheric, solar, supernova): expected **2029**.
- Beam physics with near-detector constraints: **2031**.
- **Schedule update:** First neutrino beam for oscillation physics has slipped slightly from 2028 to **2028–2029** for initial commissioning; full physics with near detector by **2031**.

### 2.5 Hyper-Kamiokande — Construction Progress

- Far-detector cavern excavation was **completed in August 2025**.
- Tank construction is ongoing through 2025–2026; PMT installation starts in **2026**.
- Operations expected in **H2 2027 to 2028**.
- J-PARC beam upgrade to 1.3 MW is proceeding.

### 2.6 LZ / XENONnT — Steady Null Results

- LZ published its 2025 world-leading limit (PRL 135, 011802) based on 4.2 tonne-years. The experiment continues taking data toward a **1000-day dataset by end of 2027**.
- XENONnT continues operations with competitive limits.
- **Trinity relevance:** Null WIMP results remain consistent with Trinity’s "no standard WIMP" prediction.

### 2.7 DESI / Euclid — Cosmology Tightens

- **DESI DR2** (March 2025) tightened the ΛCDM neutrino-mass bound to **Σm_ν < 0.064 eV** (95% CL), ruling out the inverted hierarchy at >2σ.
- This is consistent with Trinity’s prediction of a **normal mass hierarchy**.
- The apparent DESI preference for negative Σm_ν in strict ΛCDM is understood as a model-dependent degeneracy with dark energy; bounds relax in w₀wₐCDM.
- **Euclid DR1** is expected **October 2026**.

### 2.8 HL-LHC — LS3 Imminent

- LHC Run 3 ends in **2026**.
- Long Shutdown 3 (LS3) will install HL-LHC hardware through **2030**.
- Run 4 physics start confirmed for **June 2030**.
