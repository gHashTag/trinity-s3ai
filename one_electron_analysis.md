## 1. Historical Context

### 1.1 Wheeler's Original Idea (1940)

In the spring of 1940, John Archibald Wheeler called his former graduate student Richard Feynman with a startling proposition: "Feynman, I know why all electrons have the same charge and the same mass." When Feynman asked why, Wheeler replied: "Because, they are all the same electron!"

Wheeler's One-Electron Universe postulates that:
- All electrons and positrons are a SINGLE electron moving back and forth in time
- An electron moving forward in time appears as an electron
- The same electron moving backward in time appears as a positron
- A slice through spacetime at any moment intersects the electron's world-line many times, creating the appearance of many "particles"

The mathematical foundation is CPT symmetry: C (charge conjugation), P (parity), T (time reversal). Feynman formalized this in his 1949 paper "The Theory of Positrons," stating: "An electron moving backward in time is a positron."

### 1.2 Stueckelberg's Independent Discovery

Swiss physicist Ernst Stueckelberg independently developed the "zigzag world-line description of pair annihilation" at the same time, showing that:
- Pair creation = the electron being scattered forward in time
- Pair annihilation = the electron being deflected back in time
- The entire universe of charged leptons is one continuous world-line with many "kinks"

### 1.3 Why the Idea Was Abandoned

The one-electron universe faced three main objections:
1. **Electron excess:** There are far more electrons than positrons. Wheeler speculated that missing positrons might be hidden within protons.
2. **No predictive power:** The theory couldn't calculate any physical quantity.
3. **Quantum field theory:** QFT successfully describes electrons without needing the one-electron hypothesis.

However, the idea's core insight -- that all electrons are fundamentally identical because they ARE the same entity -- remained compelling and influenced Feynman's path integral formulation.

### 1.4 Modern Revival

The one-electron universe has experienced a revival through several developments:
- **Topological quantum field theory:** World-lines as braids in 3+1D TQFT (Chern-Simons theory)
- **String theory:** Single string vibrating in different modes = different particles
- **E8 theory:** Lisi's (2007) proposal that all SM particles fit in E8's 248 generators
- **Quasicrystal models:** Twarock and Patera's H4 affine extensions for quasicrystal growth
- **Pure Monist Formulation (PMF):** Recent derivation of SM from E8 quasicrystal projection
