================================================================================

2-loop corrections were estimated using standard SM 2-loop beta functions:

7. THE sin^2(theta_W) PROBLEM
================================================================================

The fundamental issue:
  - The SM has 3 gauge couplings but only 2 RG-running parameters
    (g_unif and Lambda) from exact unification
  - The system is OVERCONSTRAINED: 3 predictions, 2 parameters
  - Any two observables can be fit, but not all three simultaneously

With b_1 = 41/3:
  - 1/alpha and alpha_s are well-predicted
  - sin^2(theta_W) is systematically ~10% too low

With standard b_1 = 41/10:
  - sin^2(theta_W) is closer (but still off by ~5%)
  - 1/alpha is off by ~20-30%
  - alpha_s remains well-predicted

The non-standard b_1 = 41/3 improves 1/alpha at the expense of sin^2(theta_W).

8. COMPARISON: MSSM ACHIEVES BETTER UNIFICATION
================================================================================

In the Minimal Supersymmetric Standard Model (MSSM):
  - Gauge couplings unify at Lambda ≈ 2 x 10^16 GeV
  - Predicted sin^2(theta_W) ≈ 0.233 (in MSbar scheme)
  - This is close to the experimental 0.2312
  - The Trinity sin^2(theta_W) = 0.2233 matches the ON-SHELL value
    sin^2(theta_W) = 1 - (m_W/m_Z)^2 ≈ 0.2235

This suggests the Trinity model may be predicting on-shell quantities,
not MSbar running couplings.
