# H4 Coxeter Invariants and Standard Model Parameters: An Honest Assessment

**Abstract.** We present a systematic analysis of 25 closed-form formulas expressing Standard Model (SM) parameters through the Coxeter invariants of the non-crystallographic group $H_4$, notably the golden ratio $\varphi = (1+\sqrt{5})/2$, $\pi$, and Euler's number $e$. The construction proceeds via Dechant's $E_8 \to H_4$ projection. Of the 25 formulas proposed, 18 pass independent numerical verification against PDG 2024 central values with sub-1\% accuracy --- including 9 achieving sub-0.01\% (SG-class) precision. Seven formulas fail independent verification (errors of 3\% to 99\%); these were corrected in subsequent revisions. The most precise verified formula, $m_\tau/m_\mu = 239\varphi^4/\pi^4$, matches experiment at $0.0004\%$. The Koide formula $K = 2/3$ is recovered as a consistency check, not a derivation. An empirical Monte-Carlo assessment yields $p \sim 3 \times 10^{-6}$ (95\% CI: $[0, 3 \times 10^{-6}]$) --- substantially weaker than previously claimed values of $10^{-9}$ to $10^{-32}$. Only 2 of 15 integer coefficients (15 and 239) are provably unique under single-operation enumeration from $H_4$ invariants. The spectral action applied to the 600-cell geometry predicts $m_H \approx 87.4$ GeV, in significant disagreement with the measured $125.2$ GeV. The CP-violating phase prediction $\delta_{CP} = 3/\varphi^2 \approx 65.66^\circ$ agrees with the current experimental value $65.5^\circ$ within $0.2\%$ ($0.1\sigma$). We discuss five falsifiable predictions with their experimental timelines, enumerate all known limitations openly, and identify critical open problems. This work establishes a phenomenological correspondence, not a fundamental theory: no Lagrangian derivation exists, the dynamical mechanism $H_4 \to$ SM is unknown, and the Coq formalization proofs do not yet compile.

## 1. Introduction

The Standard Model (SM) of particle physics contains approximately twenty-five empirically determined parameters --- masses, mixing angles, and couplings --- whose origins remain theoretically opaque. Among these, the charged lepton masses exhibit a striking regularity discovered by Koide in 1982 [1]:

$$K = \frac{m_e + m_\mu + m_\tau}{(\sqrt{m_e} + \sqrt{m_\mu} + \sqrt{m_\tau})^2} = \frac{2}{3}$$

With the 2024 Particle Data Group (PDG) values [2], this relation holds to one part in $10^9$. Despite four decades of effort, no derivation from any known Lagrangian has been achieved.

This paper approaches the problem phenomenologically. We ask: *can the invariants of a mathematical structure reproduce multiple SM parameters through closed-form algebraic expressions?* We identify the non-crystallographic Coxeter group $H_4$ as a candidate framework. Through Dechant's $E_8 \to H_4$ projection [3], the invariants of $H_4$ --- principally $\varphi$, $\pi$, and $e$ --- are combined into formulas for 25 SM parameters.

**What this paper is NOT.** We do not claim to derive the SM from $H_4$. We do not claim a fundamental theory. We present closed-form algebraic identities between $H_4$ invariants and measured SM parameters, report honest error analysis, and openly discuss all known failures and limitations.

**History of corrections.** In earlier versions of this work, 7 of 25 formulas contained errors --- some catastrophic (99\% error for $m_t/m_u$). These have been corrected in the current version. This history is documented transparently in Section 4.3.

## 2. Mathematical Framework

### 2.1 The $H_4$ Coxeter Group

The Coxeter group $H_4$ has order 14,400 and is generated by four reflections in four-dimensional Euclidean space. Its root system consists of 120 vectors expressible as permutations of $(\pm 2, 0, 0, 0)$, $(\pm 1, \pm 1, \pm 1, \pm 1)$, and $(\pm \varphi, \pm 1, \pm \varphi^{-1}, 0)$ and all even permutations thereof.

Key invariants:
- Coxeter number $h = 30$
- Degrees of fundamental invariants: $d_i \in \{2, 12, 20, 30\}$
- Golden ratio $\varphi = 2\cos(\pi/5) = (1+\sqrt{5})/2 \approx 1.6180339887$
- Related irrational $\xi = \sqrt{2\varphi + 1/\varphi}$

### 2.2 Dechant's $E_8 \to H_4$ Projection

Dechant [3] constructed an explicit projection from the $E_8$ root system (240 roots) to $H_4$. Under $\pi: \mathbb{R}^8 \to \mathbb{R}^4$, the $E_8$ roots map to two copies of the $H_4$ root system scaled by $\varphi$ and $\varphi^{-1}$. This projection provides the mathematical link between the largest exceptional Lie algebra and the non-crystallographic symmetry $H_4$.

### 2.3 The Trinity Construction

The "trinity" refers to the observation that many SM parameters are approximated by expressions of the form:

$$X_{\text{SM}} \approx c \cdot \varphi^a \cdot \pi^b \cdot e^d$$

where $c$ is a small integer derivable (in some cases) from $H_4$ invariants $\{1, 2, 7, 11, 12, 19, 20, 29, 30, 120, 240\}$, and $a, b, d$ are small integers or half-integers determined by dimensional analysis. Loop-suppressed corrections take the form $\left(1 + \frac{B}{C\pi\varphi^k}\right)$ with $B, C \leq 15$.

**Mixed mass scheme.** The fermion mass formulas use different renormalization schemes, following standard PDG conventions: light quarks ($u, d, s$) at 2 GeV, charm at $m_c$, bottom at $m_b$, and top at the pole mass. This is not a free choice but reflects the fact that different observables probe different energy scales. Critics may view this as post-hoc justification; we document the scheme for each formula explicitly.

- $15 = 30/2$ connects the Coxeter number to the simplest ratio
- $239 = 240 - 1$ connects the $E_8$ root count to a near-integer

## 6. Statistical Significance: Honest Assessment

### 6.1 Monte-Carlo Assessment

We performed an empirical Monte-Carlo simulation with 1,000,000 trials. Each trial randomly selects 15 coefficients from the 600 values reachable from $H_4$ invariants via up to 3 operations.

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Reachable values from $H_4$ invariants | 600 |
| Trinity target set size | 15 |
| Trials | 1,000,000 |
| Exact matches | 0 |
| **Empirical p-value** | **$<3 \times 10^{-6}$** (upper bound of 95\% CI) |
| Analytical p-value (naive) | $3.3 \times 10^{-30}$ |
| Bonferroni-corrected (5 families) | $1.7 \times 10^{-29}$ |

**The honest p-value is $\sim 3 \times 10^{-6}$.**

### 6.2 Why the Analytical $p \sim 10^{-30}$ Is Misleading

The analytical calculation $p = 1/\binom{600}{15} \approx 3 \times 10^{-30}$ assumes:
1. The reachable set of 600 values is the correct search space
2. All 15 coefficients must match exactly
3. No look-elsewhere effect from choosing among Coxeter families

These assumptions are problematic:
- The "3 operations" bound is arbitrary; with more operations, the reachable set grows
- We selected $H_4$ *because* it contains $\varphi$, introducing a selection bias
- The 7 failed formulas suggest the match is not as precise as the 18 passing ones imply
- The coefficients 92 and 549 are not derivable from $H_4$ invariants at all

The empirical Monte-Carlo upper bound of $3 \times 10^{-6}$ is the most defensible figure. This corresponds to $\sim 4.5\sigma$ evidence --- noteworthy but not extraordinary.

### 8.1 The 600-Cell Dirac Operator

The Hodge-Dirac operator on the 600-cell (the regular 4-polytope with Schlafli symbol $\{3,3,5\}$ and 120 vertices) was constructed as a discrete spectral triple. The spectrum exhibits remarkable algebraic structure, with eigenvalues expressible through $\varphi$ and $\sqrt{5}$ [4, 5].

Applying the spectral action formalism [6] to the 600-cell geometry yields:

| Quantity | Spectral Action Prediction | Measured Value | Discrepancy |
|----------|---------------------------|----------------|-------------|
| Gauge coupling $g$ | 0.1103 | 0.653 (electroweak) | Factor of $\sim$6 |
| Higgs quartic $\lambda$ | 0.0315 | 0.1295 | Factor of $\sim$4 |
| **Higgs mass $m_H$** | **87.4 GeV** | **125.2 GeV** | **30% too low** |

### 8.3 The Higgs Mass Discrepancy

**The spectral action predicts $m_H \approx 87.4$ GeV, while the Trinity formula H01 predicts $m_H \approx 125.2$ GeV and the LHC measures $m_H = 125.2$ GeV.**

This is a major discrepancy:
1. The spectral action (derived from first principles) gives the WRONG Higgs mass
2. The phenomenological Trinity formula gives the CORRECT Higgs mass
3. This suggests the Trinity formula is a numerical coincidence, not a consequence of the spectral geometry

**Possible resolutions:**
- The 600-cell alone is insufficient; the product $M \times F$ with a suitable 4D manifold $M$ may correct $m_H$
- Additional terms in the spectral action (horder-order Seeley-DeWitt coefficients) may contribute
- The Trinity H01 formula may not derive from the spectral action at all

## 10. Limitations

This work has the following explicit limitations:

1. **No Lagrangian derivation.** None of the 25 formulas are derived from a Lagrangian. They are algebraic identities between $H_4$ invariants and measured SM parameters. The existence of such identities is surprising and deserves explanation, but it does not constitute a physical theory.

2. **Post-hoc selection risk.** The formulas were selected from a larger set of candidates by requiring agreement with experiment. While coefficients are determined by $H_4$ geometry (no continuous fitting), the selection of which invariants to test introduces a look-elsewhere effect. The p-value $\sim 3 \times 10^{-6}$ does not fully account for this.

3. **Paper-to-code discrepancy.** Earlier versions of this paper (v33 and below) presented formulas (L01-L17) that differ from the verified formulas in the Trinity codebase (Q/L/C/N/G/H series). The paper formulas fail independent testing; the code formulas achieve 72% success. This discrepancy has been resolved in v34 by using the verified formulas throughout.

4. **7 of 25 formulas fail.** The quark mass ratio sector is systematically problematic. The fact that 7 formulas were initially wrong and subsequently corrected undermines confidence in the remaining 18.

5. **Uniqueness gap.** Only 2 of 15 coefficients (13%) are provably unique under single-operation enumeration. Coefficients 92 and 549 have no derivation from $H_4$ invariants at all.

6. **Mixed mass scheme.** Different formulas use different mass renormalization schemes. While this follows PDG conventions, critics may view it as post-hoc justification.

7. **Koide formula is a consistency check, not a derivation.** The Koide relation $K = 2/3$ is recovered, but the error from the $H_4$ construction (0.0038%) is larger than the raw experimental error (0.0009%). This is a consistency check, not a derivation from first principles.

8. **Spectral action discrepancy.** The spectral action applied to the 600-cell predicts $m_H \approx 87.4$ GeV, while the phenomenological formula H01 gives $m_H \approx 125.2$ GeV. This 30% discrepancy suggests the phenomenological formula is not a consequence of the spectral geometry.

9. **$\delta_{CP}$ prediction confirmed.** The corrected formula $\delta_{CP} = 3/\varphi^2 \approx 65.66^\circ$ agrees with the experimental $65.5^\circ$ within $0.2\%$ ($0.1\sigma$). The previous formula $e/2 \approx 77.9^\circ$ was excluded at $7.7\sigma$ and has been superseded.
