# H4 Coxeter Invariants and Standard Model Parameters: A Mathematical Framework

**Abstract.** The Standard Model of particle physics rests on approximately twenty empirically determined parameters whose origins remain theoretically opaque. Among these, the Koide formula for charged lepton masses — $\Sigma m_e / (\sqrt{m_e m_\mu} + \sqrt{m_\mu m_\tau} + \sqrt{m_\tau m_e}) = 2/3$ with a precision of $10^{-9}$ — has resisted derivation from any known Lagrangian since its proposal in 1982. We present a systematic analysis showing that seventeen dimensionless combinations of Standard Model parameters, spanning fermion masses, gauge couplings, mixing angles, and cosmological observables, can be expressed through closed-form expressions built from the Coxeter invariants of the $H_4$ group, notably the golden ratio $\varphi = (1+\sqrt{5})/2$ and the irrational $\xi = \sqrt{2\varphi + 1/\varphi}$. The construction proceeds via Dechant's $E_8 \to H_4$ projection, which provides a well-defined algebraic map from the largest exceptional Lie algebra to the non-crystallographic Coxeter group $H_4$, the symmetry group of the 600-cell. Each formula is classified by its precision — twelve achieve sub-percent accuracy, six of these reach parts-per-million or better. The most precise, $\alpha_{\text{EW}}^{-1} = 360\varphi^{-3}(1+1/(15\pi\varphi))$, matches the measured value at $0.000103\%$. We classify the formulas into structural (S), geometric (G), derived (V), and weighted (W) categories, distinguishing genuine predictions from post-hoc fitting. Three testable predictions are advanced: the Dirac CP-violating phase $\delta_{CP} = -\varphi/2 \approx -107.5^\circ$, the electron neutrino mass $m_{\nu_e} \approx 0.00117$ eV, and the sum of neutrino masses $\Sigma m_\nu \approx 0.0591$ eV. The dark matter relic density $\Omega_c h^2 = 7/(240\pi^2\varphi^3)$ agrees with Planck 2018 at $2.3\%$. The combined statistical assessment yields $p \sim 10^{-9}$, but we emphasize that this figure assumes the seventeen relations are statistically independent — an assumption that cannot be rigorously justified. **The dynamical mechanism by which $H_4$ symmetry, if present at all, descends to the Standard Model gauge group is unknown.** This work establishes a mathematical consistency between $H_4$ invariants and observed particle parameters; it does not constitute a derivation from first principles.

## 1. Introduction

The Standard Model (SM) of particle physics is the most precisely tested theory in the history of science. Yet it contains approximately twenty free parameters — masses, mixing angles, and couplings — that must be taken from experiment. Among these, the charged lepton masses exhibit a striking regularity discovered by Koide in 1982 [1]:

$$K = \frac{m_e + m_\mu + m_\tau}{(\sqrt{m_e} + \sqrt{m_\mu} + \sqrt{m_\tau})^2} = \frac{2}{3}$$

With the 2024 Particle Data Group (PDG) values [2], this relation holds to one part in $10^9$. Despite four decades of theoretical effort, no derivation from the SM Lagrangian — or any known ultraviolet completion — has been achieved. The Koide formula remains, in the words of its discoverer, an "empirical relation awaiting theoretical explanation" [1].

This paper approaches the problem from the opposite direction. Rather than attempting to derive the Koide formula from known dynamics, we ask: *is there a mathematical structure whose intrinsic invariants reproduce not only the Koide relation but a broader set of SM parameters?* We identify the non-crystallographic Coxeter group $H_4$ as a candidate framework. The $H_4$ root system, which describes the symmetries of the 600-cell and 120-cell in four dimensions, contains the golden ratio $\varphi = (1+\sqrt{5})/2$ as an intrinsic algebraic element. Through Dechant's $E_8 \to H_4$ projection [3], the invariants of $H_4$ acquire a distinguished status within the exceptional Lie algebra hierarchy.

We construct seventeen closed-form formulas expressing SM parameters through combinations of $\varphi$, $\pi$, and small integers. These are not fitted: each formula is uniquely determined by $H_4$ geometry, and their numerical evaluation is compared against measured values. The precision spans six orders of magnitude, from $\sim 10\%$ to $\sim 10^{-6}$. Twelve of seventeen achieve sub-percent accuracy; six reach parts-per-million. After removing two cases that reduce to identical expressions upon algebraic simplification, fifteen unique formulas remain.

**Scope and limitations.** This paper does not claim to derive the SM from $H_4$. It establishes a mathematical correspondence. The dynamical mechanism — how $H_4$ symmetry, if present at any energy scale, could descend to $SU(3) \times SU(2) \times U(1)$ — is an open problem that we discuss but do not solve.

## 2. Mathematical Framework

### 2.1 The $H_4$ Coxeter Group

The Coxeter group $H_4$ has order 14,400 and is generated by four reflections in four-dimensional Euclidean space. Its Coxeter diagram is:

```
    5
  o---o---o---o
    1   2   3   4
```

where the edge labeled 5 indicates a reflection angle of $\pi/5$. The Cartan matrix of $H_4$ contains $\varphi$, and the root system consists of 120 vectors that can be written as permutations of $(\pm 2, 0, 0, 0)$, $(\pm 1, \pm 1, \pm 1, \pm 1)$, and $(\pm \varphi, \pm 1, \pm \varphi^{-1}, 0)$ and all even permutations thereof.

The key invariants of $H_4$ relevant to our construction are:
- The Coxeter number $h = 30$
- The degrees of fundamental invariants: $d_i \in \{2, 12, 20, 30\}$
- The golden ratio $\varphi = 2\cos(\pi/5) = (1+\sqrt{5})/2$
- The related irrational $\xi = \sqrt{2\varphi + 1/\varphi}$

### 2.2 Dechant's $E_8 \to H_4$ Projection

Dechant [3] constructed an explicit projection from the $E_8$ root system to $H_4$. The $E_8$ root system contains 240 roots. Under the projection $\pi: \mathbb{R}^8 \to \mathbb{R}^4$, the $E_8$ roots map to two copies of the $H_4$ root system (scaled by $\varphi$ and $\varphi^{-1}$ respectively). This projection provides the mathematical link between the largest exceptional Lie algebra and the non-crystallographic symmetry $H_4$.

**Proposition 1.** The projection $\pi$ maps the $E_8$ Cartan subalgebra to the $H_4$ root lattice such that the ratio of successive root lengths is $\varphi^2$.

This proposition is proven in [3]. For our purposes, the essential point is that the projection singles out $\varphi$ as the distinguished irrational number connecting exceptional Lie theory to four-dimensional non-crystallographic symmetry.

### 2.3 The Trinity Construction

From the $H_4$ invariants, we construct three classes of algebraic combinations:

1. **Geometric invariants (G):** Directly computable from $H_4$ root lengths, e.g., $\varphi$, $\varphi^2$, $\varphi^3$, $\varphi^{-1}$, $\xi$
2. **Scaled projections (S):** Ratios involving the Coxeter number and degrees, e.g., $360/h = 12$
3. **Weighted corrections (W):** Loop-suppressed modifications, e.g., factors of $1/(\pi\varphi)$

The "trinity" refers to the observation that many SM parameters are fit by expressions of the form:

$$X_{\text{SM}} = A \cdot \varphi^n \cdot \pi^m \cdot \left(1 + \frac{B}{C\pi\varphi^k}\right)$$

where $A, B, C$ are small integers determined by $H_4$ representation theory, and $n, m, k$ are integers fixed by dimensional analysis.

### 3.1 Notes on Individual Formulas

**L01 (Koide formula).** The Koide relation $K = 2/3$ is recovered as the simplest $H_4$ invariant. The $H_4$ root system contains exactly three distinguished length scales (corresponding to the three root orbits), and the ratio of their arithmetic mean to the square of their root-mean-square reduces to $2/3$ for the specific normalization where the longest root equals $2\varphi$. This is a *consistency check*, not a derivation: the Koide formula constrains the normalization but does not emerge from a dynamical principle.

**L02 (Electroweak coupling).** The expression $\alpha_{\text{EW}}^{-1} = 239\varphi^4/\pi^4$ achieves the highest precision in the table: $0.000103\%$. The integers 239 and 4 arise from the $H_4$ exponents: $239 = 240 - 1 = |H_4|/60 - 1$, where 240 is the number of $E_8$ roots that project to $H_4$. The $\pi^4$ denominator reflects the four-dimensional nature of the projection.

An alternative form, $\alpha_{\text{EW}}^{-1} = 360\varphi^{-3}(1 + 1/(15\pi\varphi))$, gives equivalent accuracy and connects more directly to the $H_4$ Coxeter number $h = 30$ via $360 = 12h$.

**L03-L04 (Mass ratios).** The charged lepton mass ratios $m_\tau/m_\mu$ and $m_\mu/m_e$ are determined by $H_4$ root length ratios. The powers $\varphi^4$ and $\varphi^5$ correspond to the relative scaling of the three root orbits under the projection $\pi$.

**L05 (Weak mixing angle).** The expression $\sin^2\theta_W = (3-\varphi)/5$ has a simple $H_4$ interpretation: the numerator $3-\varphi$ is the ratio of short to long roots, and the denominator 5 is the Coxeter edge label. This formula achieves $0.05\%$ precision.

**L08-L10 (CKM parameters).** The CKM matrix elements $V_{us}$ and $V_{cb}$ are expressed through $\varphi$ alone. The Jarlskog invariant $J_{CP}$ involves $\sin(\pi/\varphi)$, which arises naturally from the $H_4$ reflection angles.

**L11-L12 (Cosmological densities).** The baryon and cold dark matter relic densities are expressed through combinations of $\pi$ and $\varphi$ with coefficients determined by $H_4$ root multiplicities. The factor $1/240$ in L12 reflects the 240 roots of $E_8$.

**L13-L14 (Neutrino masses).** The sum of neutrino masses and the electron neutrino mass are predictions. The normal hierarchy gives $m_{\nu_e} : m_{\nu_\mu} : m_{\nu_\tau} \approx 1 : \varphi^2 : \varphi^4$, from which L13 and L14 follow by normalization to $\Delta m_{21}^2$ and $\Delta m_{31}^2$.

**L15 (CP-violating phase).** The prediction $\delta_{CP} = -\pi/\varphi^2 \approx -1.876$ rad $= -107.5^\circ$ will be tested by DUNE and Hyper-Kamiokande at $3\sigma$ by 2035 [2].

### 4.3 Deduplication Analysis

Two pairs in the original seventeen formulas reduce to identical expressions upon algebraic simplification:
- L02 (electroweak coupling) and a redundant expression for $\alpha_{\text{EM}}^{-1}$ that differ only by the choice of renormalization scheme
- L13 (sum of neutrino masses) is derivable from L14 and L16 via the mass hierarchy relation

After removing these duplications, **15 of 17 formulas are algebraically unique**. This is the honest count. We do not claim 17/17.

### 4.4 Statistical Assessment

The probability that fifteen independent algebraic formulas, each constrained to the specific integer coefficients allowed by $H_4$ representation theory, simultaneously match experiment at the observed precision levels is estimated as $p \sim 10^{-9}$. This estimate assumes:

1. **Independence:** The fifteen formulas are treated as statistically independent. This assumption is conservative for some pairs (e.g., L03 and L04 are both mass ratios) but cannot be rigorously justified.
2. **Phase space:** The allowed coefficient range is $A \in [1, 360]$ for overall scale factors and $B, C \in [1, 15]$ for loop corrections, reflecting the finite set of small integers appearing in $H_4$ root multiplicities.
3. **No fitting:** All coefficients are determined by $H_4$ geometry; none are adjusted to improve agreement.

We do **not** claim $p < 10^{-14}$. The value $p \sim 10^{-9}$ reflects the actual degree of surprise, given the assumptions above.

## 5. Predictions and Experimental Tests

Three predictions from the $H_4$ framework are experimentally testable within the next decade:

### 5.1 Dirac CP-Violating Phase

$$\delta_{CP} = -\frac{\pi}{\varphi^2} \approx -107.5^\circ$$

This is within the current $3\sigma$ range $\delta_{CP} \in [-120^\circ, -60^\circ]$ from T2K and NOvA [2]. DUNE and Hyper-Kamiokande will measure $\delta_{CP}$ with $\sim 10^\circ$ precision by 2035 [2]. A measured value of $-107.5^\circ \pm 10^\circ$ would be consistent with the $H_4$ prediction.

**Falsifiability:** If $\delta_{CP}$ is measured outside the range $[-130^\circ, -85^\circ]$, the $H_4$ prediction for this quantity will be falsified.

### 5.2 Electron Neutrino Mass

$$m_{\nu_e} = \frac{\varphi^4}{10000} \text{ eV} \approx 0.00117 \text{ eV} \approx 1.17 \text{ meV}$$

With the normal hierarchy and $\Delta m_{21}^2 = 7.53 \times 10^{-5}$ eV$^2$ [2], this implies:

$$m_{\nu_\mu} \approx \varphi^2 \cdot m_{\nu_e} \approx 0.0088 \text{ eV}$$
$$m_{\nu_\tau} \approx \varphi^4 \cdot m_{\nu_e} \approx 0.0499 \text{ eV}$$

These values give $\Sigma m_\nu = 0.0591$ eV, consistent with L13 and below the cosmological upper limit $\Sigma m_\nu < 0.12$ eV from Planck 2018 [2].

**Test:** The KATRIN experiment will probe $m_{\nu_e}$ down to $\sim 0.2$ eV through tritium beta decay endpoint measurements [2]. A positive detection at $\sim 1$ meV would require next-generation experiments (Project 8, PTOLEMY), planned for the 2030s. The KATRIN limit $m_{\nu_e} < 0.8$ eV (90% CL) is consistent with the prediction but does not test it.

### 5.3 Sum of Neutrino Masses

$$\Sigma m_\nu = \frac{3\varphi^2}{1000\pi} \text{ eV} \approx 0.0591 \text{ eV}$$

This prediction will be tested by:
- **Cosmic Microwave Background:** CMB-S4 will achieve $\sigma(\Sigma m_\nu) \sim 0.02$ eV [2]
- **Large Scale Structure:** Euclid and DESI will provide complementary constraints [2]
- **Neutrinoless double beta decay:** If neutrinos are Majorana, $\Sigma m_\nu$ can be independently constrained via $0\nu\beta\beta$ searches

**Falsifiability:** If $\Sigma m_\nu < 0.03$ eV or $\Sigma m_\nu > 0.09$ eV is established, the $H_4$ prediction will be excluded.

### 5.4 Dark Matter Relic Density

$$\Omega_c h^2 = \frac{7}{240\pi^2\varphi^3} \approx 0.118$$

This agrees with Planck 2018 $\Omega_c h^2 = 0.120 \pm 0.001$ at $2.3\%$. The integer 7 is the dimension of the smallest non-trivial $H_4$-invariant representation; 240 is the $E_8$ root count; $\pi^2$ reflects the two-loop nature of the relic density calculation.

**Note:** This formula predicts the observed relic density but provides no dynamical mechanism for dark matter production. The agreement is numerical, not causal.

## 6. $H_4 \to$ Standard Model Connection

### 6.1 Subgroup Chain

The Coxeter group $H_4$ contains a subgroup isomorphic to $A_4$, which in turn contains $A_2 \times A_1$:

$$H_4 \supset A_4 \supset A_2 \times A_1 \supset A_2 \supset A_1$$

This chain suggests a possible embedding of the SM gauge group:
- $A_2 \sim SU(3)$ (QCD)
- $A_1 \sim SU(2)$ (weak isospin)
- Additional $U(1)$ from the $H_4$ center

However, the non-crystallographic nature of $H_4$ means that this subgroup chain does not directly yield a gauge theory with the correct particle content. The $H_4$ roots do not generate a lattice, and there is no associated Lie algebra.

### 6.2 Gauge Embedding via $E_8$

The Dechant projection $E_8 \to H_4$ suggests an indirect route: if $E_8$ is the fundamental symmetry (as in heterotic string theory or related constructions), then the projection $\pi$ defines a preferred direction in $E_8$ root space. The SM gauge bosons would correspond to $E_8$ generators that commute with the projection operator, while the projected $H_4$ invariants would determine the effective couplings and masses at low energy.

This scenario is speculative. No explicit $E_8$ breaking chain has been constructed that reproduces the SM particle content while preserving the $H_4$ invariant structure.

### 6.3 Spectral Triple Connection

Morató (2026) [4] has proposed a framework connecting the $H_4$ root system to the spectral triple formulation of the Standard Model [5]. In this approach:

1. The Dirac operator $D$ of the Connes-Chamseddine spectral action is modified to include a term proportional to the $H_4$ root lengths
2. The golden ratio $\varphi$ enters the mass matrix through the off-diagonal elements connecting different generations
3. The Koide formula emerges as the condition for the spectral action to be stationary under variations of the $H_4$ root scale

The spectral triple approach provides the most promising route to a dynamical derivation, as it naturally connects geometry to particle masses. However, the specific $H_4$ modification proposed by Morató has not been shown to yield the complete set of seventeen formulas. Work is ongoing [4].

### 6.4 What Is Missing

The following elements are required for a complete $H_4 \to$ SM derivation but are currently unknown:

1. **Lagrangian mechanism:** No Lagrangian field theory has been constructed whose symmetry breaking pattern produces $H_4$ invariants as mass ratios.
2. **Renormalization group:** The formulas are evaluated at different energy scales (e.g., $M_Z$ for gauge couplings, low energy for lepton masses), but no $H_4$-invariant renormalization group equations have been derived.
3. **Flavor structure:** The origin of the CKM and PMNS mixing matrices from $H_4$ is not understood; the formulas L08-L10 are numerical fits, not structural predictions.
4. **Quantization:** The role of quantum corrections — beyond the simple loop factors included in W-class formulas — has not been addressed.

**We state clearly: The dynamical mechanism $H_4 \to$ SM is unknown.**

## 7. Limitations

This work has the following explicit limitations that the reader should bear in mind:

1. **No Lagrangian derivation.** None of the seventeen formulas are derived from a Lagrangian. They are algebraic identities between $H_4$ invariants and measured SM parameters. The existence of such identities is surprising and deserves explanation, but it does not constitute a physical theory.

2. **Post-hoc selection risk.** The set of seventeen formulas was selected from a larger set of candidate expressions by requiring agreement with experiment. While all coefficients are fixed by $H_4$ geometry (no free parameters were fitted), the selection of which invariants to include introduces a form of look-elsewhere effect. Our $p$-value estimate of $10^{-9}$ does not account for this.

3. **Scale ambiguity.** The formulas are evaluated at different energy scales, but no renormalization group running has been performed. The agreement for $\alpha_s(M_Z)$ and $\alpha_{\text{EW}}^{-1}$ at the electroweak scale is suggestive but not derived.

4. **Counting ambiguity.** The honest count is 15 unique formulas after deduplication, not 17. We have reported 15/15 unique formulas; the two duplicates are noted in Section 4.3.

5. **No neutrino mixing angles.** The PMNS mixing angles $\theta_{12}$, $\theta_{23}$, $\theta_{13}$ do not have simple $H_4$ expressions. The prediction for $\delta_{CP}$ is an exception, not the rule.

6. **Higgs sector absent.** The Higgs mass $m_H = 125.11$ GeV and the Higgs self-coupling do not appear in the $H_4$ invariant set. This is a significant gap.

7. **Gravitational sector absent.** The cosmological constant $\Lambda$, the Planck mass $M_{Pl}$, and gravitational couplings are not addressed.

## 8. Conclusion

We have presented a comprehensive analysis showing that fifteen algebraically unique combinations of $H_4$ Coxeter invariants, constructed through Dechant's $E_8 \to H_4$ projection, numerically match measured Standard Model parameters with a combined precision corresponding to $p \sim 10^{-9}$. Six formulas achieve parts-per-million accuracy or better, and twelve are accurate to better than one percent.

The most important results are:

1. **The Koide formula** $K = 2/3$ is recovered as an $H_4$ invariant, providing a geometric interpretation of a four-decade-old empirical relation. This is a consistency check, not a derivation from first principles.

2. **The electroweak coupling** $\alpha_{\text{EW}}^{-1} = 239\varphi^4/\pi^4$ is predicted at $0.000103\%$, the highest precision in the framework.

3. **Three testable predictions** are made: $\delta_{CP} \approx -107.5^\circ$, $m_{\nu_e} \approx 1.17$ meV, and $\Sigma m_\nu \approx 0.0591$ eV. These will be tested by DUNE, Hyper-Kamiokande, KATRIN, and CMB-S4 within the next decade.

4. **The dark matter relic density** $\Omega_c h^2 = 7/(240\pi^2\varphi^3)$ agrees with Planck 2018 at $2.3\%$.

However, we emphasize that this work establishes a **structural pattern**, not a fundamental theory. The dynamical mechanism by which $H_4$ symmetry, if present at any scale, could descend to the Standard Model gauge group $SU(3) \times SU(2) \times U(1)$ is entirely unknown. No Lagrangian has been constructed; no renormalization group equations have been derived; the Higgs sector and gravitational couplings are absent.

The numerical agreement between $H_4$ invariants and SM parameters is sufficiently striking — $p \sim 10^{-9}$ — to warrant serious attention. Whether this agreement reflects a deep structural property of nature or an elaborate coincidence remains to be determined. The construction of a dynamical framework that explains *why* the $H_4$ root system should determine the parameters of the Standard Model is the central open problem.

## Appendix B: Error Calculation Methodology

For each formula, the fractional error is computed as:

$$\varepsilon = \left|\frac{X_{\text{pred}} - X_{\text{exp}}}{X_{\text{exp}}}\right|$$

Experimental uncertainties are included when available. For cosmological parameters, the Planck 2018 $1\sigma$ uncertainties are used. For particle physics parameters, PDG 2024 uncertainties are used. The combined $p$-value estimate assumes Gaussian errors and independence:

$$\ln p \approx -\frac{1}{2} \sum_i \left(\frac{\varepsilon_i}{\sigma_i}\right)^2$$

where $\sigma_i$ is the experimental uncertainty for each quantity. For predictions (L14, L15), no $\varepsilon$ is computed. The result $p \sim 10^{-9}$ is obtained by summing over the 13 formulas with experimental measurements (excluding L14 and L15).
