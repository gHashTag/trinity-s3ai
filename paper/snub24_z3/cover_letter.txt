Submission of: "Z_3 tripartition of the snub 24-cell as a flavour structure"

To: Journal of Mathematical Physics / arXiv math.GR

Dear Editor,

We submit for your consideration the paper "Z_3 tripartition of the snub
24-cell as a flavour structure" by the Trinity S^3AI Collaboration.

The paper proves a purely combinatorial theorem about the snub 24-cell,
a chiral uniform 4-polytope with 96 vertices. We show that these 96 vertices
decompose canonically into three sets of 32 under the free left action of
a particular Z_3 subgroup of the binary tetrahedral group 2T. The proof is
group-theoretic and elementary: we identify the vertices with the difference
set 2I \ 2T, construct an explicit order-3 generator g in 2T, and prove that
its left action on the 96 coset representatives is fixed-point-free.

The theorem has been independently machine-verified in two proof assistants:
- Coq (file Snub24CellZ3.v, commit d883275)
- Lean 4 (file Snub24Z3.lean, commit 916bc6e)
Both formalisations are available in the ancillary materials and in our
public repository.

We emphasise that this is a combinatorial theorem, not a full derivation of
the three-generation structure of the Standard Model. We do not claim to
explain Yukawa couplings, the CKM matrix, or neutrino mixing. The paper
contains an explicitly labeled "Physical speculation" section that honestly
assesses the obstacles to physical interpretation.
