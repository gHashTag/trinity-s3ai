4. H4 PREDICTION FOR UNIFICATION SCALE
================================================================================
   H4 invariants:
     φ = (1+√5)/2 ≈ 1.618  (golden ratio)
     h = 30                  (Coxeter number)
     |H4| = 120              (binary icosahedral group order)

   Testing H4-inspired formulas:

     Λ = M_Pl / φ^15  = 8.95 × 10^15 GeV  ≈ 0.4 × Λ_GUT^SUSY
     Λ = M_Pl / φ^20  = 8.07 × 10^14 GeV  ≈ 0.8 × 10^15 GeV (SU(5) GUT)
     Λ = M_Pl / h^2   = 1.36 × 10^16 GeV  ≈ 0.6 × Λ_GUT^SUSY

   The formula M_Pl / φ^15 ≈ 9 × 10^15 GeV is within a factor ~2-4

   The exponent 20 = 2 × (number of roots of H4 = 60)/6 = 2×10 is not
   directly meaningful in the H4 structure.

   VERDICT: H4 invariants can parametrize the GUT scale, but do not
   uniquely predict it. The φ^n scaling is suggestive but needs
   theoretical justification.

5. GRAVITATIONAL CORRECTIONS
================================================================================
   Gravitational operators: δ(1/α_i) ~ c_i × Q²/M_Pl²

   At Q = 10^16 GeV:  δ(1/α) ~ 10^-6  (negligible)
   At Q = 10^18 GeV:  δ(1/α) ~ 10^-2  (potentially relevant)
   At Q = M_Pl:       δ(1/α) ~ 1       (significant)

   For SUSY GUT at ~10^16 GeV, gravitational corrections are negligible.
   They only become important near the Planck scale.

   H4 → E8 embedding: The 120 roots of H4 are a subset of E8's 240 roots.
   E8 × E8 heterotic string theory naturally has unification at M_Pl.
   If H4 is embedded in E8, the "true" unification scale is M_Pl,
   with effective SUSY-scale unification arising from φ-suppressed
   splitting.

   VERDICT: H4 embedded in E8 suggests Planck-scale unification with
   effective lower-scale unification via φ-suppression factors.

================================================================================

   may probe τ_p ~ 10^35 years, getting closer to the prediction.

   SU(5) comparison: With Λ_GUT = 10^15 GeV, τ_p ≈ 10^32 years,
   which is EXCLUDED by experiment.

FINAL VERDICT
================================================================================

H4-DERIVED GAUGE COUPLINGS DO NOT UNIFY AT A SINGLE POINT.

The critical failure mode: The H4-derived U(1)_Y coupling g1(m_Z) = 0.357
is 40% lower than the measured value (g1^measured ≈ 0.463). This causes
the α1 running to miss the α2-α3 unification by a huge margin.

However:
→ The α2 and α3 couplings match experimental values and unify well in SUSY
→ The α1 discrepancy is the sole obstruction to full H4 unification  
→ If α1 were corrected to its measured value, SUSY unification works
→ The φ^n suppression of M_Pl gives scales close to SUSY GUT

STATUS: EXPERIMENTAL → CONSISTENT (with the caveat that α1 must be fixed)

If H4 predicts g1(m_Z) = 0.357 as a fundamental input, then H4 is
inconsistent with gauge coupling unification. But if H4 allows a
corrected g1 that matches the measured value, then unification proceeds
as in standard SUSY GUTs with Λ_GUT ~ 2×10^16 GeV.
