## PART 1: THE KOIDE RELATION AND ITS DESCENDANTS

### Entry 1: Koide 1982 — Original Lepton Mass Formula

- **Authors:** Yoshio Koide
- **Year:** 1982
- **Title:** "Fermion-Boson Two Body Model of Quarks and Leptons and Cabibbo Mixing"
- **Journal:** Lettere al Nuovo Cimento, Vol. 34, p. 201
- **Key claim:** The charged lepton masses satisfy the remarkable empirical formula:
  $$K = \frac{m_e + m_\mu + m_\tau}{(\sqrt{m_e} + \sqrt{m_\mu} + \sqrt{m_\tau})^2} = \frac{2}{3}$$
  Koide proposed this relation based on a fermion-boson composite model. He used the then-imperfectly-known electron and muon masses to predict the tau mass as 1776.97 MeV.
- **Relation to Trinity:** The 2/3 coefficient is the foundational mystery. Trinity's triple-phase decomposition echoes the three-term structure and the geometric interpretation (45-degree angle in mass space).
- **Citation:** Y. Koide, Lett. Nuovo Cimento **34**, 201 (1982)

## PART 2: BARUT AND NAMBU MASS FORMULAS

### Entry 12: Nambu 1952 — Alpha-Quantization of Masses

- **Authors:** Yoichiro Nambu
- **Year:** 1952
- **Title:** (Empirical mass formula based on fine-structure constant)
- **Journal:** Published in conference proceedings and referenced in subsequent literature
- **Key claim:** Proposed that elementary particle masses are quantized in units related to the fine-structure constant alpha:
  $$m = \frac{N \cdot m_e}{2\alpha}$$
  where N is a positive integer and m_e is the electron mass. For N = 27, the formula yields a mass close to the proton mass. The formula generates mass quanta: 35, 70, 105, 140 MeV, etc. This was called "alpha-quantization" by MacGregor.
- **Relation to Trinity:** Nambu's formula establishes the foundational connection between particle masses and the electromagnetic coupling constant alpha, which Trinity extends to the full SM parameter set.
- **Citation:** Y. Nambu, reported in Y. Nambu, Prog. Theor. Phys. (1952); see also references in M. H. MacGregor, "The Power of Alpha" (2007)

### Entry 13: Barut 1979 — Lepton Mass from Magnetic Self-Energy

- **Authors:** A. O. Barut
- **Year:** 1979
- **Title:** "Lepton Mass Formula"
- **Journal:** Phys. Rev. Lett. **42**, 1251 (1979)
- **Key claim:** Postulated that a quantized magnetic self-energy of magnitude (3/2) alpha^(-1) n^4 m_e is added to the electron rest mass to generate heavier leptons:
  $$M(N) = M_e \left(1 + \frac{3}{2\alpha^{-1}} \sum_{n=0}^{N} n^4\right)$$
  For n=1: m_\mu ≈ 206.55 m_e (excellent agreement)
  For n=2: m_\tau ≈ 1786.08 MeV (close to experimental 1776.99 MeV)
  Predicted a fourth lepton M_\delta = 10293.7 MeV.
- **Relation to Trinity:** Barut's formula connects lepton mass differences directly to alpha and integer quantum numbers. The n^4 scaling (later debated as n^2) provides a template for understanding mass generation from electromagnetic self-interaction.
- **Citation:** A.O. Barut, Phys. Rev. Lett. **42**, 1251 (1979)
